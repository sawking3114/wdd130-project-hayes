About

this site is an informational webpage about the histories and differences of wiki types. The website compares different wiki types, has guides on how to make wikis, and includes the history of how wikis came to be.

wiki.gg

Wiki.gg is a wiki-hosting platform focused on gaming communities, offering streamlined tools for contributors and clean, ad-light pages for readers. It provides a stable, modern backend designed to handle large, active fanbases without the clutter found on some other platforms. Because wiki.gg is community-driven rather than ad-heavy or corporate-run, many gaming communities feel they have more control over their content. This emphasis on performance, contributor freedom, and minimal monetization sets it apart from many other wiki hosts.

fandom.com

Fandom is one of the largest wiki-hosting platforms, known for supporting thousands of fan communities across entertainment, gaming, and pop culture. It offers built-in tools, social features, and a highly branded interface that helps connect readers to related fandom content. Compared to many other wiki hosts, Fandom relies heavily on ads and a unified site design, which gives it a distinct look and feel but can be more cluttered. Its massive network, cross-community visibility, and centralized ecosystem make it stand out from smaller or more specialized wiki platforms.

miraheze.org

Miraheze is a non-profit wiki-hosting platform that provides free, customizable wikis for a wide range of communities and topics. It emphasizes transparency, open-source values, and user control, allowing communities to tailor their sites far more than many corporate-run platforms. Unlike hosts that rely on ads, Miraheze is funded entirely through donations, keeping its pages clean and uncluttered. Its commitment to community governance and flexible configuration makes it a unique option among modern wiki platforms.

wikidot.com

Wikidot is a long-standing wiki platform that lets users create highly customizable sites with flexible page layouts and modular components. It features a built-in scripting system and template tools that allow communities to design pages with more structure and complexity than many traditional wiki engines. Unlike hosts focused on purely collaborative editing, Wikidot blends wiki functions with website-building elements, giving it a more hybrid feel. This combination of customization, structured content tools, and site-like design options sets it apart from many other wiki platforms.
